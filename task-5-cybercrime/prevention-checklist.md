# Stay Safe Online: A Prevention Checklist for College Students

As digital citizens, it's our responsibility to navigate the online world securely. Use this checklist to protect your personal information and digital identity.

### Essential Security Measures
- [ ] **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using easy-to-guess passwords like "password123", your pet's name, or your birthdate. Use a secure password manager and different passwords for each account.
- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Always turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and academic accounts to add a crucial secondary layer of security.
- [ ] **Beware of Phishing Links:** Do not click on unverified links sent via SMS, WhatsApp, or email, particularly those demanding urgent action or suspiciously claiming you've won a prize.
- [ ] **Update Software Regularly:** Keep your smartphone's operating system, laptop's antivirus software, and all applications updated to patch known security vulnerabilities.
- [ ] **Think Before You Share:** Limit the amount of personal data (like your live campus location, personal phone number, or graduation year) you share on public social media profiles.
- [ ] **Use Secure Wi-Fi Networks:** Avoid conducting sensitive computing or logging into personal accounts while connected to public, unsecured Wi-Fi networks (like those in cafes or dorm lounges).

### Financial & UPI Safety
- [ ] **Never Enter Your UPI PIN to Receive Money:** A UPI PIN is *only* ever required to send money or check your bank balance. If someone asks you to enter it to "receive a refund" or "collect cash," it is definitely a scam.
- [ ] **Verify the Receiver's Details:** Before authorizing any payment on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm, explicitly check the verified receiver's name or merchant ID that appears on your screen.

### How to Report Cybercrimes in India
If you or a peer fall victim to an online fraud or cybercrime, act immediately to minimize damage:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** File an official complaint online at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** Call **1930** immediately to report financial frauds and initiate the freezing of fraudulent transactions.
